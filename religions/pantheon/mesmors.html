Mesmors is known as the Goddess of Death and the Harvest. It is said that she is responsible for the cycle of life and death, and that she oversees the transition of souls from the mortal world to the afterlife.

Mesmors is often depicted as a regal, austere figure, with piercing green eyes and long, dark hair. She is said to be fiercely protective of the souls under her care, and will go to great lengths to ensure that they are properly guided to their final resting place.

In some cultures, Mesmors is also associated with agriculture and farming, as she is believed to oversee the harvest and ensure that the crops are healthy and bountiful. Farmers and rural communities may offer prayers or offerings to her during the harvest season, in the hopes of receiving her blessings and ensuring a successful harvest.
