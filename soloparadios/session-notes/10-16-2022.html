Session Prep

During the prior session, the party was introduced to the concept of a new force corrupting monsters to make them more aggressive. Meeting Lias, the party was told of a recent string of attacks by hyper-aggressive monsters. The party was told that the recent attacks killed several locals, including Kymil, the previous captain of the guard.

Lias will provide the crew with a detailed rundown of events thus far after introducing the current captain of the guard, Vanali:

"During the last several weeks, Monster attacks have claimed the lives of no less than two dozen elves. The previous captain of the guard, Kymil, was among those lost. We have increased our patrol three-fold with the assistance of volunteers, and have near-daily attacks at this point.

Nargacuga prowl the edges of the forest, snatching elves on their way to visit family. Anjanath have burned homes and families-"

At this point, Venali will interrupt,[1]

“Suffice it to say, losses have been heavy, and time is of the essence.”

Every day, there is an ever-rising chance of an attack on the city again. Starting with a d20, roll a dice at the end of each day, lowering the ‘size’ of the dice each day until an attack occurs, then resetting the sequence. On a roll of 1, frenzied monsters will attack the city, with civilians likely in danger. The lowest possible chance is a d4 for this. This puts a “ticking clock” component into play, with the constant risk of attack looming, but without disrupting the investigation too much.

Example:

  1. Day 1, d20 roll = 15, no attack
  2. Day 2, d12 roll = 4, no attack
  3. Day 3, d8 roll = 1, attack
  4. Day 4, d20 roll

Potential Encounters

Every day spent in the forest, players roll a d12. On a 1, roll on the d8 table below to determine encounter. Re-roll repeats.

  1. 4x Great Jagras
  2. Fey Riddles
  3. Nightshade Paolumu
  4. Lit Candle
  5. Pair of Anjanath
  6. Sunny Meadow
  7. Rathalos w/ Pack of Bulfango (running from Rathalos, hit and run attacks from Bullfango before Rathalos engages)
  8. Time Loop Forest Grove

Encounter Details

Fey Riddles

PCs encounter a fey creature who challenges them with a simple riddle. Answering the riddle correctly will result in a small boon and no further action taken. Rolling incorrectly will result in a curse. Refusing the riddle will also result in a curse.

Lit Candle

PCs come around to a single, lit candle. If they put out the flame by any means, magical darkness devours 300 ft. radius around the now unlit candle. A pair of shadow materializes out of the smoke, attacking the party. If the candle is lit, the shadow, and the darkness disappear. The shadow won't pursue the party out of the darkness covered area.

Sunny Meadow

720 ft (220 meter) area, appears to be sunlit meadow. DC 15 perception check, to notice it's way more bright then it should be. At the center of the area is a chest, holding a Rare magical item. Anybody who enters, or ends their turn in the area suffers 1d6 radiant damage. (The only time double damage is taken is on initially entering the area.)

Time Loop Forest Grove

Every 6 seconds trees grow, fall, decompose, repeat. If a tree lands on someone, grows under someone, or just hits someone with a branch then they take the appropriate damage.
